Jump to Recipe Introduction

Harira is a warm, hearty, nutritional Moroccan soup consisting of lentils and chickpeas, and loaded with fresh herbs, warm spices, and veggies. It makes for the ultimate comfort food and is great for the colder months.

The Ingredients

Olive oil will add healthy fat and help the aromatics infuse.

Water will be what we cook the lentils and chickpeas in until they are tender.

Chickpeas will add health fat, plant protein, and fiber, helping keep blood sugar levels stable.

Lentils also add protein and fiber to avoid spiking blood sugar.

Crushed tomatoes add color and a tangy, rich base.

Tomato paste will strengthen the tomato base.

Spice blend: Salt, pepper, turmeric, cumin, cinnamon, and cayenne make the flavor profile of this dish warm, rich, savory, and well-balanced.

Aromatics: Garlic and onion serve as the major aromatics, subtly infusing with the other spices bring the dish together.

Veggies: Carrots and celery add color, texture, vitamins, and minerals, along with mild hints of each.

Cilantro adds bright flavors and color.

Lemon: Serve this dish with a lemon wedge to add a bright flavor.

Substitutes & Additives

Lime can be served with this dish instead of lemon.

Veggies: Use what you have.

Meats: Adding meat like lamb, chicken, or pork would work well.

Chicken stock can be used instead of water to add more flavor depth.

Health Benefits

Harira is very nutrient-rich and boasts several health benefits.

Diets (Is it diet-friendly?)

Mediterranean - YES: Legumes, tomatoes, herbs, and oil are favored in the Mediterranean diet.

Keto - MAYBE: Traditional harira is high in carbs due to the lentils and beans. Consider leaving out the chickpeas, lentils, and carrots and using kale or spinach and celery with meat instead.

Ketovore - NO: Too high in carbs, and contains grains.

Carnivore - NO: The carnivore diet consists almost entirely of animal products: meat, fish, eggs, and some dairy. It excludes all plant foods, including grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes.

Paleo - NO: This recipe contains legumes.

Vegan - YES: Many versions of harira are naturally vegan, including this one.

Gluten-Free - MAYBE: If you don't use flour as a thickener, and if you use certified gluten-free spices, this will be gluten-free.

Low-FODMAP - NO: This recipe contains chickpeas, onion, and garlic.

Diabetics - MAYBE: Add a meat for protein, and do less lentils and carrots, and eat a small serving.

SAD - YES: Nearly everything is allowed in the Standard American Diet.

Directions

Step 1:

Chop/mince:

0.5 medium yellow onions

0.5 large carrots

1 celery stalks

0.333 cilantro (original - 1/3 cup)

1 large garlic clove, minced

Set aside.

Step 2:

Add the onions, carrots, and celery to a large pot with:

2 TBSP extra virgin olive oil

Cook until softer on medium-low heat (about 5 minutes).

Step 3:

Now add the garlic and:

0.5 tsp pepper

0.5 tsp turmeric

0.5 tsp cumin

0.125 tsp cinnamon (original - 1/8 tsp)

0.125 tsp ground cayenne (original - 1/8 tsp)

Stir everything together well.

Step 4:

Now add the chopped cilantro and:

4 cups water

1 14.5oz cans chickpeas, drained

0.667 dry lentils (original - 2/3 cup)

1 15.5oz cans crushed tomatoes

1 TBSP tomato paste

Stir everything together well.

Bring to a boil then reduce to a simmer on medium-high. Cover and let the lentils cook until tender.

For dry lentils, it will take around an hour to cook. You might need to add more water at the end, so keep an eye on the lentils.

Step 5:

Salt to taste, and slice:

l lemons (into wedges)

Serve with lemon wedges and enjoy!
