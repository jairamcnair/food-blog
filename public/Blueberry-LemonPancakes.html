Jump to Recipe Introduction

These pancakes are so easy to make and taste heavenly. This recipe would be a show-stopper for a friend's brunch or special weekend breakfast, especially for lemon lovers!

The Ingredients

All-purpose flour will create a classic, slightly chewy texture.

White sugar will create a light, soft, and sweet pancake.

Baking powder is a leavening agent that will create carbon dioxide gas bubbles, to make baked goods rise, puff up, and become light, tender, and fluffy instead of dense and flat.

Salt brings out the flavor and balances out the sweetness.

Olive oil adds moisture and softness. Make sure to use regular, neutral tasting olive oil, not extra-virgin olive oil, which will be too bitter.

Vanilla extract enhances the pancakes flavor and sweetness and pairs great with the lemon.

Lemon zest gives the pancakes their strong lemon flavor

Lemon juice helps enhance the lemon flavor and soften the pancake by enhancing the baking powder's role

Blueberries add color and a wonderful taste that pairs deliciously with the lemon

Eggs provide structure and act as a binder to hold ingredients together, while also adding richness, color, and moisture.

Milk creates a richer, more tender pancake than water.

Substitutes & Additives

Butter: Top the pancakes with butter

Maple syrup: Serve topped with maple syrup, which you can heat up slightly in the microwave before pouring over the pancakes to avoid making them too cold.

Fresh berries: Serve with even more fresh berries/fruit.

Whipped cream: Serve topped with whipped cream, which you can also flavor with maple syrup/sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, etc.

Baking soda + vinegar: If you are out of baking powder, you can use baking soda with a dash of white vinegar instead (not too much to protect the flavor).

Health Benefits

Pancakes made with white flour don't boast many health benefits and are best consumed in moderation for the average adult. Consider saving them for weekends or special occasions.

Directions

Step 1:

Start pre-heating a large, non-stick skillet over medium heat.

Step 2:

In a medium or large glass measuring pitcher, add:

1 whole egg

2 TBSP olive oil

0.5 tsp vanilla extract

2 tsp lemon juice

2 TBSP (or to taste) fresh blueberries

0.333 cups whole milk

Whisk until well combined.

Step 3:

In a separate bowl, whisk:

0.5 cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp baking powder

1 tsp lemon zest

1.5 TBSP white sugar

0.25 tsp salt

Step 4:

Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients.

Fold the batter together until just combined. Leave some lumps, but not too many.

Let the batter rest for 1-2 minutes so the baking powder can create bubbles and create fluffy, tall pancakes.

The batter should be JUST runny enough to pour smoothly, but still thick enough to not make a flat pancake. If needed, add a bit more milk until this consistency is reached.

Step 5:

Pour 1/2 cup of batter out into the non-stick skillet and cook each side until golden brown. Repeat until all the batter is used up.

Be sure to adjust the heat as needed. The pancakes shouldn't brown too quickly, but also not take too long. To get a uniform, golden brown color and prevent blotchy pancakes, don't grease the skillet. The non-stick should be enough. You know a pancake is ready to flip when there are bubbles in the middle and the outer edges are cooked.

Serve and enjoy!
