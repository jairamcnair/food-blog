Jump to Recipe Introduction

If you are tired of eating chicken, don't give up until you try this recipe first!

The Ingredients

Boneless, skinless chicken breast is low in fat and high in protein, making it a great base to any meal.

Olive oil will help the spices stick to the chicken and add moisture, juiciness, and healthy fat.

Salt will bring out the other flavors.

Paprika will adds color and a warm, mildly sweet, and slight smoky depth.

Ground cumin will give a earthy, warmth.

Ground cardamom will give a slight citrusy and 'minty' undertone.

Ground cinnamon will give a savory warmth.

Crushed red pepper flakes will add a hint of spice and peppery taste.

Garlic will act as a crucial aromatic.

Fresh lime juice will give a hint of lime and help tenderize the chicken.

Substitutes & Additives

Lemon juice can be used instead of lime juice, though I prefer the subtle difference lime has to offer.

Ground coriander or allspice can be used instead of ground cardamom.

Traditional Lebanese toppings for shawarma include: Toum (Garlic Sauce), cucumber pickles or pickled turnips, shredded lettuce, tomato, onion, sumac, and tahini. In my American household, I typically use sour cream, cheese, hot sauce, pickled jalapenos, fresh cilantro, and a lime wedge.

Health Benefits

Shawarma is high in protein, and can be versatile and adjusted to many different diets.

Diets (Is it diet-friendly?)

Mediterranean - MAYBE: Refined grains are discouraged; whole grains are preferred, so eat this with whole grain bread/tortillas.

Keto - MAYBE: Use a low-carb tortilla/bread or lettuce wrap instead of regular tortillas and bread.

Ketovore - MAYBE: If you just eat the chicken.

Carnivore - NO: The carnivore diet consists almost entirely of animal products: meat, fish, eggs, and some dairy. It excludes all plant foods, including spices, grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes.

Paleo - MAYBE: This recipe can be had on the paleo diet as long as you don't use bread/tortillas.

Vegan - NO: Chicken.

Gluten-Free - MAYBE: Leave out the bread and use lettuce wraps instead. Use spice brands labeled “certified gluten-free”.

Low-FODMAP - MAYBE: Leave out the garlic.

Diabetics - MAYBE: Ditch the bread/tortilla for a lettuce wrap or low-carb version.

SAD - YES: Nearly everything is allowed in the Standard American Diet.

Directions

Step 1:

Start pre-heating a large, non-stick skillet over medium heat.

Pre-heat your oven to 425 F.

Step 2:

In a small bowl, mic:

1 tsp salt

1 tsp paprika

0.5 tsp ground cumin

0.5 tsp ground cardamom

0.25 tsp ground cinnamon

0.25 tsp crushed red pepper flakes

Set aside.

Step 3:

Flatten out to about 1-inch thick:

1 lb. boneless, skinless chicken breast

Step 4:

To the chicken, add:

2 TBSP olive oil

1 large garlic clove, minced

1 lime, juice of

Also add in the spices you mixed earlier and completely coat the chicken.

Step 5:

Sear the chicken in the heated skillet (about 1-2 min each side).

After searing, bake the chicken until the internal temperature is 165 F.

Searing will help lock the juices in and prevent the chicken from getting dry.

Step 6:

Serve with flour tortillas, lavash, or pita bread. Top with your favorite toppings and enjoy!
