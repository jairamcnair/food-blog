Jump to Recipe Introduction

Cinnamon rolls are my absolute favorite colder-month dessert. They just have that warm, cozy aesthetic so popular in fall and winter, not to mention they are a natural way to have your home smelling just like cinnamon-sugar!

The Ingredients

Yeast: Will help the dough rise and the rolls be airy.

Bread flour: Bread flour has a higher protein content than regular AP flour, so it makes the cinnamon rolls chewier.

White sugar: Used to lightly sweeten the rolls, though the major sweetener will be the icing.

Brown sugar + Cinnamon: Used in the cinnamon-sugar filling.

Salted butter: Salted butter will be used in the cinnamon-sugar filling and in the icing.

Powdered sugar: Used to make the icing.

Cream cheese: Will make the icing thick, creamy, and help balance the sweetness of the powdered sugar.

Vanilla extract: Used in the icing to give a subtle hint of vanilla.

Milk: Milk will create rich, moisturized rolls.

Eggs: Eggs will act as a binder and also add moisture and richness, and slight color.

Substitutes & Additives

Unsalted butter can be used instead of salted butter to have more control of the salt content, just be sure to add more.

All-purpose flour can be used instead of bread flour, though the rolls might not be as fluffy and chewy.

Pumpkin spice can be added to the dough to make pumpkin cinnamon rolls.

Health Benefits

Cinnamon rolls are a dessert, so who cares about the lack of health benefits!

Directions

Step 1:

In the bowl of a standing mixer, proof:

7 g active dry yeast (7g = 1 packet)

0.75 cups warm milk

Wait 5-10 minutes for the mixture to form a foam. This will prove the yeast is active.

This step is to make sue the yeast is active before making the dough so you don't have to start over if the yeast isn't active. The milk should be around 100-110 F, if it's too hot (120F) it will kill the yeast. Add a pinch of sugar to help the yeast activate.

Step 2:

In the same bowl, add:

3 cups bread flour

0.25 cups white sugar

4 TBSP melted butter (salted)

2 whole eggs

With the dough hook attachment, mix until well combined.

Step 3:

Grease a large bowl with butter or oil.

Dump the dough into the bowl and cover it with plastic wrap or a damp kitchen towel.

Let it sit at *warm* room temperature until it's doubled in size (about 1 - 1.5 hrs in a warm home).

If it's cold in your home, it will take longer for the dough to rise.

Step 4:

Once the dough has risen, mix:

0.667 cups brown sugar (original - 2/3 cup)

1.5 TBSP ground cinnamon

Step 5:

Roll the dough out into a 9-inch by 13-inch rectangle.

Using your hands or a spatula, spread 4 TBSP salted butter all over the rolled out dough.

Now sprinkle the cinnamon-brown sugar mixture over the dough and make sure it's well covered.

Step 6:

Pre-heat the oven to 350 F.

Roll the dough up (the rolled up dough should be 13 inches long).

Use a knife to score where you will cut the dough to get around 12 cinnamon rolls (or more if you increase recipe portions).

Use a thread to cut the rolls by positioning the thread underneath, crossing it over the top, and then pulling as if trying to make a knot.

Step 7:

Line a large baking sheet or dish with parchment paper.

Place the rolls on the sheet.

Bake for 20 minutes or until golden brown.

You can keep the rolls on the baking sheet/dish far apart, or closer together so they expand into each other.

Step 8:

While the rolls bake, make the icing.

In the bowl of the standing mixer, whisk:

8 oz cream cheese, softened

3 TBSP salted butter

1 cup powdered sugar

1 tsp vanilla extract

Whisk until well combined. Set aside.

I prefer my icing less sweet and more 'cream cheesy' and thick to balance out the sweetness of the powdered sugar; experiment to find what you like.

Step 9:

When the rolls are done, you can either ice them all together (if it's for an event), or ice them as you eat them (better option if you'll plan on having left overs).

Serve and enjoy!
