Jump to Recipe Introduction

Hamburger Helper is a nostalgic comfort food for lots of people, including myself. Growing up, I remember how excited I would get when we had it for dinner, and I knew there wouldn't be leftovers! One of the best things about hamburger helper is that you most likely already have everything you need to make it!

The Ingredients

Ground beef is the protein of this dish, but feel free to try it with ground turkey, chicken, lamb, pork, etc.

Tomato paste adds color and gives the recipe a strong tomato base to add onto.

Heavy whipping cream adds richness, and helps thicken the recipe.

Shredded sharp chedder adds flavor, thickens the recipe, and makes it cheesy.

Flour will help thicken the recipe to your desired consistency.

White onion and garlic are aromatics that infuse into the recipe, creating more flavor dimension.

Olive oil will help the aromatics release their flavors and infuse into the rest of the dish.

Salt and pepper: Basic seasonings

Paprika adds warm, slightly sweet notes.

Italian seasoning adds savory, herbal, slightly earthy flavor.

Crushed red pepper flakes add a bit of kick, and a warm, peppery flavor.

Pasta will be the carb of the dish.

Water will allow the flavors to marry while the pasta cooks.

Substitutes & Additives

Beef broth: You can use beef broth instead of water for added flavor, though I typically just use water.

Butter: You can use butter instead of olive oil, though I like how olive oil brings out the aromatics for this dish.

Ground poultry: Use ground turkey or chicken if you need to cut down on fat.

Nutritional yeast: Instead of shredded cheese, use nutritional yeast for a cheesy taste with significantly less fat.

Beans: Though it wouldn't technically be like traditional Hamburger Helper, you could use beans and/or lentils instead of pasta. Beans have a lower glycemic index than pasta, so they'd help keep blood sugar levels more level.

Health Benefits

This dish is very high in fat and carbs, which is not optimal for health for prolonged periods of time.

See the substitutes for ideas on how to make cuts.

Directions

Step 1:

Dice/mince:

1 cup white onion

4 tsp garlic

Tip: Mash the garlic with the side of a wide knife to peel it easier.

Step 2:

To a medium sauce pan, add the onion and:

2 TBSP olive oil

1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes

2 tsp Italian seasoning

0.25 tsp salt

Cook on medium heat until the onions are soft (about 5 minutes), then add the garlic and cook until fragrant (about 30 seconds).

Step 3:

Now to the sauce pan add:

1 lb. ground beef

0.25 tsp salt

0.25 tsp pepper

2 tsp paprika

Break up the meat and brown it completely.

Step 4:

Now add:

2 TBSP tomato paste

Mix it in well.

Step 5:

Now add:

3 cups water or beef broth

1 cup heavy cream

6 oz. pasta

Bring it to a boil, then reduce to a simmer over medium-low. Cover and simmer until done (20-25 min). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking or burning on the bottom.

6oz. of pasta is about 2 cups.

Step 6:

Once done, reduce the heat to the lowest heat setting and add:

1 TBSP flour

0.75 cups shredded sharp cheddar

Let it thicken.

Salt to taste.

Serve and enjoy!
