The Ingredients

Bread: The best breads for French toast are sturdy, thick-cut loaves like brioche, challah, or sourdough, as they absorb the custard well without falling apart. Day-old or slightly stale bread is often preferred because its drier nature allows for better absorption of the egg mixture.

Vanilla extract will add extra flavor that perfectly compliments the sugar.

Saigon cinnamon is a specific type of cassia cinnamon. Saigon cinnamon is stronger, sweeter, and more aromatic than regular Ceylon cinnamon, but it contains more coumarin, so it’s best used in moderation if you eat cinnamon daily. Ceylon is milder and safer for frequent use. If you eat cinnamon frequently or in larger amounts (like daily in coffee, oatmeal, or baking), Ceylon cinnamon (regular cinnamon) is considered safer long-term.

White sugar makes the bread sweet.

Milk will be the base of the custard and add richness.

Egg will act as a binder, creating a rich, custardy batter with milk that soaks into the bread, adding protein and fat for a tender, fluffy interior and a beautifully caramelized, golden crust when cooked

Powdered sugar is a nice garnish that adds sweetness.

Substitutes & Additives

Fresh fruit: Top your french toast with fresh fruit.

Maple syrup: Dip your french toast in maple syrup.

French toast sticks: Turn this recipe into French toast sticks by slicing the bread before dipping in the custard and frying.

Health Benefits

Traditional French toast can be a healthy, protein-packed meal, especially when made with whole-grain bread, egg whites, and low-fat milk, offering sustained energy, better fullness (satiety), muscle support, and stable blood sugar.

Since this recipe isn't traditional, and adds extra sugar, it will not boast the same health benefits.

Carnivore - NO: The carnivore diet consists almost entirely of animal products: meat, fish, eggs, and some dairy. It excludes all plant foods, including grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes.

Directions

Step 1:

In a wide-based bowl, whisk:

0.25 cups whole milk

1 whole egg

1 TBSP white sugar

0.25 tsp vanilla extract

0.25 tsp ground Saigon cinnamon

Whisk until well combined. This is the custard.

Step 2:

In another small bowl, mix:

1 TBSP white sugar

0.25 tsp ground Saigon cinnamon

This is the optional cinnamon-sugar coating for you to coat the toast in after you cook the bread.

Step 3:

Begin heating a non-stick pan over medium heat. Cut your bread into 1.5-inch by 1-inch (by however long) sticks or leave the slices whole.

Drench the slices in the milk-mixture and put them in the skillet. Cook each side until golden brown. Now (optional), coat each stick or slice in the cinnamon sugar coating you made.

Serve and enjoy! Top with your favorite toppings or eat it how it is.
