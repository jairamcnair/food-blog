Jump to Recipe Introduction

If you are tired of scrambled eggs, and you need to get creative, this recipe is for you. Soft-boiled eggs have a silky center, right in between a runny center and a hard (dry) one. Soft-boiled eggs are very versatile too. Eat them whole; mash them up and add salt and pepper; or slice them and put them on toast. Not only are they delicious and versatile, but you'll save yourself some time not having to stand at the stove in the morning.

Directions

Step 1:

Bring a pot to a boil on the highest heat.

Once the water is boiling, using a spoon with holes, gently and slowly lower the eggs in. Try to fit as many on the spoon as you can so they cook the same amount of time.

For soft boiled eggs, boil 7 minutes for a runny center; 8 minutes for a gooey center; and 9 minutes for a firm center.

Tip: Tip: The sudden temperature change can cause the eggs to break. To prevent this, you can let the eggs come to room temperature and/or when you lower the eggs into the water, leave half in and half out for around 5 seconds before slowly lowering the entire egg in.

Step 2:

Once the timer goes off, immediately run cold water over the eggs and soak them. This prevents the eggs from cooking more, and it makes peeling easier too.

If you don't soak the eggs in cold water, they will keep cooking until they are medium/hard boiled.

Once they are cool enough to handle, peel the eggs.

Tip: To make peeling even easier, dump the hot water out and shake the eggs into each other before running cold water over them.

Serve and enjoy!
